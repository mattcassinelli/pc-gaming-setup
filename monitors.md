# Gaming monitor guide (Mac + PC)

Standalone guide to choosing monitors when you already have a Mac and are adding a gaming PC. Covers resolution, refresh rate, panel type, VRR, and how to share one set of displays (or not).

**Related:** [Desk and switching](desk-and-switching.md) (KVM, input switching) · [PC specs](pc-specs.md) (GPU vs resolution) · [Checklist](checklist.md)

---

## Resolution: 1080p vs 1440p vs 4K

| Resolution | Typical size | Best for | GPU demand |
|------------|---------------|----------|------------|
| **1080p** (1920×1080) | 24–27" | High FPS, esports, budget builds | Low |
| **1440p** (2560×1440) | 27–32" | Sweet spot: clarity + performance | Medium |
| **4K** (3840×2160) | 27–32" (desk) | Max clarity; needs strong GPU | High |

- Match resolution to your **GPU** and target framerate — see [PC specs](pc-specs.md).
- On a **Mac**, 1440p and 4K scale well with Retina/HiDPI; 1080p can look soft.
- **Ultrawide** (3440×1440, 3840×1600) is an option if you want one wide display for both productivity and gaming.

---

## Refresh rate: 60 Hz → 120/144/165 Hz+

- **60 Hz** (e.g. current Dell): fine for productivity; limiting for fast games.
- **120–165 Hz**: noticeable improvement in motion clarity and responsiveness; good target for most gaming.
- **240 Hz+**: mainly for competitive/esports; diminishing returns unless you consistently hit high FPS.

For **Mac + gaming PC**, a 1440p 144 Hz or 165 Hz monitor is a common choice: good for games and still sharp for Mac work.

## Panel types

| Type | Strengths | Tradeoffs |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **IPS** | Colors, viewing angles, fast options | Some glow; contrast ~1000:1 |
| **VA** | Better contrast, deep blacks | Slower pixel response; possible smearing |
| **OLED** | Best contrast, blacks, motion | Burn-in risk; often pricier; some at 27" 1440p |

- **IPS** is the default recommendation for mixed use (work + gaming).
- **OLED** is ideal for dark rooms and cinematic games if you’re comfortable with burn-in mitigation (e.g. pixel refresh, varied content).

---

## VRR: G-Sync and FreeSync

- **Variable refresh rate** syncs the monitor to the GPU’s frame rate to reduce tearing and stutter.
- **FreeSync** (AMD): open standard; many monitors support it; works with NVIDIA in “G-Sync Compatible” mode.
- **G-Sync** (NVIDIA): original brand; “G-Sync Compatible” = FreeSync monitor certified by NVIDIA.

For **NVIDIA**: prefer “G-Sync Compatible” or native G-Sync. For **AMD**: FreeSync or FreeSync Premium. Many 144 Hz+ gaming monitors support both.

## Size and viewing distance

- **Desk:** 27" at 1440p is a common sweet spot; 32" if you sit farther back.
- **Viewing distance:** ~arm’s length for 27"; a bit more for 32".
- **Single vs dual:** One large (e.g. 32") or ultrawide vs two 27" — depends on workflow and how you share between Mac and PC (see [Desk and switching](desk-and-switching.md)).

---

## Sharing monitors between Mac and PC

- **One set of monitors:** Use monitor **input switching** (HDMI 1/2, DP 1/2) or a **KVM** to switch display + USB together — see [Desk and switching](desk-and-switching.md).
- **Dedicated per machine:** No switching; each machine has its own display(s). Uses more desk space and budget.
- **Inputs:** Prefer **HDMI 2.1** and/or **DisplayPort 1.4** for 1440p/4K at high refresh (check monitor specs for max resolution/Hz per input).
