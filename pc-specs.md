# PC specs (research targets)

Standalone guide to choosing a gaming PC when you already have a Mac: GPU tiers by resolution, CPU/RAM/storage, prebuilt vs build-your-own, and budget tiers.

**Related:** [Monitors](monitors.md) (resolution/Hz vs GPU) · [Checklist](checklist.md)

---

## GPU tiers by resolution

Match the GPU to your **target resolution and framerate** (and to your monitor — see [Monitors](monitors.md)).

### 1080p (1920×1080)

- **Goal:** High FPS (100+), esports, or budget build.
- **Typical cards (current gen or last gen):** RTX 4060, RX 7600, RTX 3060.
- **VRAM:** 8 GB is usually enough for 1080p.

### 1440p (2560×1440) — common sweet spot

- **Goal:** 60–144+ FPS at high settings; good balance of clarity and performance.
- **Typical cards:** RTX 4070 Super, RX 7800 XT, RTX 5070; used market: RX 6800 XT.
- **VRAM:** 12 GB recommended for 1440p at high/ultra.

### 4K (3840×2160)

- **Goal:** 4K 60+ FPS; often with upscaling (DLSS/FSR).
- **Typical cards:** RTX 4090, RTX 4080 Super, RTX 5090/5080; AMD RX 7900 XTX.
- **VRAM:** 16 GB+ preferred for 4K and future titles.

### Quick reference

| Resolution | Min VRAM | Example GPUs (research current pricing) |
|------------|----------|----------------------------------------|
| 1080p      | 8 GB     | RTX 4060, RX 7600, RTX 3060            |
| 1440p      | 12 GB    | RTX 4070 Super, RX 7800 XT             |
| 4K         | 16 GB+   | RTX 4080 Super, RX 7900 XTX, RTX 4090  |

## CPU, RAM, storage

- **CPU:** For gaming, a mid-tier current gen (e.g. Ryzen 5/7, Core i5/i7) is usually enough; focus more budget on GPU. If you stream or do heavy multitasking, step up.
- **RAM:** **16 GB** minimum; **32 GB** comfortable for gaming + background apps and future titles.
- **Storage:** **NVMe SSD** for OS and games (faster load times); add a second SSD or HDD for bulk storage if needed. 500 GB–1 TB for OS + several games is a common start.

## Prebuilt vs build-your-own

| | Prebuilt | Build-your-own |
|---|----------|----------------|
| **Pros** | Warranty, one support contact, no assembly | Choice of every part; often better value; upgrade path clear |
| **Cons** | Often pricier; some proprietary parts; bloatware | Time and research; you handle support and assembly |
| **When to buy** | Sales (Black Friday, Prime Day, back-to-school); check reviews for thermals and noise | When you have a target budget and part list; compare to prebuilt deals |

Use **r/buildapc** and **PC part picker** (or similar) to compare builds and prebuilts at your budget.

---

## Budget tiers (indicative)

- **Entry:** 1080p focus; enough for 60+ FPS in most games; prebuilts often $600–1,000 (prices vary).
- **Mid-range:** 1440p sweet spot; 60–144 FPS at high settings; often $1,000–1,800.
- **High-end:** 4K or 1440p ultra/high FPS; $1,800+.

Sales (Black Friday, Prime Day, New Year, new product launches) often shift these bands; use current guides and Reddit for up-to-date recommendations.
