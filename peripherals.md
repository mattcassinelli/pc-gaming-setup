# Peripherals (keyboard, mouse, headset)

Standalone guide to keyboards, mice, and headsets when using both a Mac and a gaming PC: shared vs dedicated, compatibility, and connection types.

**Related:** [Desk and switching](desk-and-switching.md) (KVM vs Barrier; one set vs two) · [Checklist](checklist.md)

---

## Shared vs dedicated

- **One set shared:** One keyboard, one mouse (and optionally one headset) used for both Mac and PC. Switching via **KVM** (hardware) or **Barrier** (software) — see [Desk and switching](desk-and-switching.md). Cuts cost and clutter; single muscle memory.
- **Dedicated per machine:** Separate keyboard/mouse (and headset) for Mac and PC. No switching logic; more gear and cables. Makes sense if you strongly prefer different layouts (e.g. Mac layout vs gaming keyboard) or want to leave each machine “ready” without any switch.

---

## Keyboard

- **Layout:** Mac has Option/Command vs Windows Alt/Win. Many keyboards work on both; keys may be labeled for one OS. You can remap in OS or with keyboard software.
- **Mechanical vs membrane:** Mechanical is common for gaming (tactile, durable); membrane is quieter and cheaper. Choose by feel and noise.
- **Compatibility:** Most USB (and many Bluetooth) keyboards work on both Mac and PC. **Mac-specific keys** (e.g. Touch ID, Mission Control) only work on Mac unless the keyboard has a mode switch.
- **Connection:** **Wired USB** is simplest with a KVM (one cable to the KVM). **Bluetooth** can pair to both machines but usually only one at a time unless the keyboard supports multi-device switching (e.g. K380). **Wireless dongle** — one dongle per machine or move the dongle when switching (or plug dongle into KVM if the KVM supports USB).

---

## Mouse

- **DPI/sensitivity:** Adjust per game and per OS; many gaming mice have on-the-fly DPI buttons and software (on PC; check Mac support).
- **Compatibility:** USB (wired or dongle) works on both. Bluetooth mice often support multi-device (pair to both, switch via button). With a **KVM**, plug the receiver (or cable) into the KVM so one mouse serves both machines.
- **Mac vs PC:** Extra buttons and software (e.g. Logitech G Hub, Razer Synapse) may have limited Mac support; check before buying if you rely on them on Mac.

---

## Headset

- **Shared use:** Plug into the **KVM’s USB** (if it has a headset port) so audio + mic follow the active machine. Or use a **USB sound card / DAC** that’s plugged into the KVM. Alternatively, **dedicated headset per machine** if you don’t want to switch.
- **Connections:** **USB** — single plug for audio + mic; easy with KVM. **3.5 mm** — separate mic/headphone or combo; you’d need the KVM to carry analog audio or use the machine’s front/side jacks and switch the cable. **Wireless (dongle)** — plug dongle into KVM so one headset works on both (if the headset supports one dongle).
- **Mac compatibility:** Most USB and 3.5 mm headsets work. Wireless dongles sometimes need a Mac driver or have fewer features on Mac; check manufacturer support.

---

## Connection types (recap)

| Type        | KVM-friendly     | Mac + PC note                    |
|-------------|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Wired USB** | Yes (plug into KVM) | Works on both; no pairing        |
| **Wireless dongle** | Yes (dongle in KVM) | One dongle, switch via KVM       |
| **Bluetooth** | No (per-machine) | Pair to each; switch in OS or on device |

For **one keyboard + one mouse + one headset** across Mac and PC, **wired USB** or **wireless dongle into the KVM** is the simplest.
